### project 1

Truth and Landscape

### Project Description

Presentation:
The Culture of the Natural

<iframe src="https://docs.google.com/presentation/d/e/2PACX-1vSdvFZJVH1QDBkQznyeYfCoYYaKwBpt54xIMWDMt8hsVsVEOMmOvaT1_uNDb0GiCdtyRfhfu0c43R9Y/pubembed?start=false&loop=false&delayms=3000" frameborder="0" width="960" height="749" allowfullscreen="true" mozallowfullscreen="true" webkitallowfullscreen="true"></iframe>

Objective:

- This assignment challenges you to explore and critically engage with landscape photography by examining the relationship between nature and culture. You can explore in two different ways:

1. through the historical aesthetic concepts such as the Beautiful, the Picturesque, and the Sublime, or,
2. by responding to these themes and reflect on Robert Adams’ essay _Truth and Landscape_.

Shots and Images:

- Take at least 100 shots, out of which you'll be creating 3 final images.

Assignment Guidelines:

1. _Research & Reflection_: Begin by reading Robert Adams’ essay Truth and Landscape. In his writing, Adams critiques the romanticized and idealized representations of landscape, emphasizing the importance of honesty in how we depict the natural world. Consider how this perspective influences the way you think about landscape photography today.

2. _Nature and Culture in Landscape_: As you compose your images, reflect on the tension between untouched nature and human-altered landscapes. Can we ever separate nature from culture? How does your environment reflect this interaction? Consider photographing landscapes that show evidence of human presence—suburban developments, parks, highways, or even industrial sites. Alternatively, explore spaces where nature seems dominant but may still bear subtle marks of human influence.

3. The Beautiful, the Picturesque, and the Sublime: Using these three historical categories of landscape aesthetics as a framework, take three separate images that represent each concept:

- _The Beautiful_: Emphasizes balance, and a severe order. Evokes power and eternity.

- _The Picturesque_: Features a sense of variety and an artfully composed but slightly irregular scene, akin to a painterly composition. Seek out dynamic framing, contrasting textures, and engaging perspectives. Is an illusion of the natural hidden behind a great deal human led work.

- _The Sublime_: Captures the overwhelming power of nature—stormy skies, powerful light, mountains and hills, and rugged, dramatic landscapes that evoke awe or insignificance in the face of nature’s force.

OR: _Truth and Landscape_: explore Robert Adams ideas of autobiography, geography, and metaphor:

- Geography:A record of what a location looked like at a specific time.

- Autobiography: Insights into the photographer's own experiences, feelings, and what they deemed important.

- Metaphor: The potential for the image to point toward larger, universal stories or meanings beyond the physical subject matter.

4. _Technical Considerations_: Use your camera settings intentionally to build your ideas. Consider how depth of field, exposure, and focal length impact the way your landscape is perceived. Experiment with composition—foreground, middle ground, and background—to add depth and meaning to your images. Think about the work of the photographers and painters from the lecture.

5. _Editing & Post-Processing_: While Adams emphasized honesty in landscape photography, consider how digital post-processing can enhance or distort truth in an image. Reflect on your choices—does editing bring out the reality of a place, or does it create an idealized vision?

Submission Requirements:

- Take 100 shots - upload a screen grab of the imported images (unedited).
- Three final images, each exploring one of the following: the Beautiful, the Picturesque, and the Sublime.

- A 300-word reflection discussing how your 3 final images relate to Robert Adams’ ideas on truth in landscape, the idea of the sublime, beautiful or picturesque, as well as the balance between nature and culture in your compositions.

Evaluation Criteria:

- Conceptual: Thoughtful engagement with the themes of the assignment.
- Visual: Technical execution and creative composition.
- Reflection: Depth of reflection in your written component.

### Project Description

Presentation:
Evocative Objects

<iframe src="https://docs.google.com/presentation/d/e/2PACX-1vSK5Up3l5-zELklSpdd2de601U8hvnPT7_Rnvnc8UP7HK-O0D2e1jxklQ3Xgv93ujRsnc0JFgkJ1z4k/pubembed?start=false&loop=false&delayms=3000" frameborder="0" width="960" height="569" allowfullscreen="true" mozallowfullscreen="true" webkitallowfullscreen="true"></iframe>

Objective:
Shoot 35 frames for a still life. Reference the still life lecture for idea for:

- the meaning of objects – their metaphorical and conceptual associations
- the use of light – creating visual hierarchy and other associations
- the composition – the arrangement of the objects, what is near to what, creating space
- the framing – where you, the viewer, are and how that changes meaning in the objects
- create images with variation in objects, light, composition and framing.

Submission:
upload a zip file with 35 raw images

### Project 3

Portrait

### Project Description

Presentation:
Creative Portraiture

<iframe src="https://docs.google.com/presentation/d/e/2PACX-1vQfBSlU04BBikB9CupIzzk_A8-deQMVLtdUqP1BXzpf7X3U1bn9HE5OHiQtCFL2XB9jn3FnmWFxiwCs/pubembed?start=false&loop=false&delayms=3000" frameborder="0" width="960" height="569" allowfullscreen="true" mozallowfullscreen="true" webkitallowfullscreen="true"></iframe>

Conduct a photo shoot. Photograph 35 exposures of a single subject (a person), your choice.

The idea for this assignment is to actively explore your subject with your camera. Your 35 shots should be as different as possible in terms of framing of the environment, distance, perspective, light, point of view, focus, scale, etc. Think about varying the ways you are using your camera - not just the pose of your model or the subject of your photos, for example. A portrait is as much as about your work behind the camera as the subject's in front of it.

Submission:
upload a zip file with 35 raw images
